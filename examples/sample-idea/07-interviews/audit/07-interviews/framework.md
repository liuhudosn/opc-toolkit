**2–3 moments most likely to generate deflection and suggested follow-up probes:**

1. **Q7 (Have you ever missed billable hours…)** – Deflection risk: Respondents may deny or minimize missed hours to appear competent.  
   → Probe deeper: “Even the most careful freelancers occasionally realize a few minutes slipped through. Think back to a time when you looked at your log and felt you’d worked more than it showed—what did you notice first?”

2. **Q12 (Tell me about a time when an invoicing error or delay directly affected a client relationship or your cash flow)** – Deflection risk: Socially desirable answers may downplay the incident or shift blame.  
   → Probe deeper: “It’s totally normal for mistakes to happen. What did the client actually say when you discussed the error, and how did that conversation make you feel about your system afterwards?”

3. **Q13 (What’s the part that makes you want to scream…)** – Deflection risk: The emotional framing may cause the interviewee to laugh it off or give a superficial “everything’s fine” response.  
   → Probe deeper (after a neutral rewrite): “Walk me through the last time that tedious part took you longer than expected—what exactly happened, minute by minute, from when you started until you finally finished?”
