## freelance-developer

## Opening
1. Can you walk me through a typical workweek as a solo freelancer? I’m interested in how you split your time between coding, communication, and admin.
   → Probe: When you say “admin,” what does that include for you?
   → Probe: Can you recall the last time admin tasks popped up unexpectedly and disrupted your flow?

2. Tell me about your most recent project from start to finish — from the initial client conversation to the final payment you received.
   → Probe: At what point did you start tracking your time, and what did that process look like?
   → Probe: What’s the one step in that journey you’d redo if you could?

## Problem Context
3. Think back to the last time you sent an invoice. How did you pull together the billable hours and client details?
   → Probe: Can you walk me through that, step by step?
   → Probe: What felt like unnecessary back-and-forth between tools or documents?
   → Probe: Did anything get missed or misestimated that time? How did you catch it?

4. Tell me about the most recent instance where you lost track of time you spent on a project, even if it was just 15 minutes.
   → Probe: What led to that? Was it a specific tool, distraction, or a client request?
   → Probe: How did you discover the missing time? What did you do about it?
   → Probe: Has that happened before this year? Roughly how often?

5. When was the last time you had to switch between three or more different tools or apps just to get the admin side of a project done? Walk me through that.
   → Probe: Which switch felt the most jarring? Why?
   → Probe: What’s the one thing that if it were combined, would have saved you a step?

6. Recall a recent situation where you had to juggle time tracking across multiple clients on the same day. How did you keep them separate and accurate?
   → Probe: Did you ever start tracking for the wrong client? What fixed that?
   → Probe: How much mental energy did context-switching between their time-trackers take?

7. Have you ever missed billable hours because of how you track them? Tell me about the most recent time that happened.
   → Probe: What did that mistake cost you — in dollars, time, or trust?
   → Probe: Did you adjust your system after that? What changed?

## Current Behavior
8. Walk me through your current stack for tracking time, taking project notes, and creating invoices. Which tools do you use and how do they connect?
   → Probe: What’s the most manual step in that workflow?
   → Probe: Why did you choose this particular combination? What did you try before?

9. Have you ever tried a different approach or tool that promised to simplify time-to-invoice? What made you abandon it?
   → Probe: What was the breaking point? One big frustration or death by a thousand cuts?
   → Probe: How long did you give it before switching back?

10. How do you currently handle retainer clients versus one-off project clients in your time tracking and billing?
    → Probe: Is there a difference in tools or processes? Show me the fork in the workflow.
    → Probe: Which type of client makes you dread admin the most?

## Impact & Severity
11. In the past month, roughly how many hours did you spend on non‑billable tasks related to tracking time, organizing project notes, and getting invoices out?
    → Probe: What did that time steal from — coding, rest, family time, or business development?
    → Probe: Can you recall a specific instance where admin overran and you had to rush a deliverable or miss a deadline?

12. Tell me about a time when an invoicing error or delay directly affected a client relationship or your cash flow.
    → Probe: How did the client react? What did you tell them?
    → Probe: What did that do to your confidence in your system?

13. What’s the one part of this whole time‑to‑billing process that makes you want to scream every time you do it?
    → Probe: How often does that happen? Every week, every month?
    → Probe: Have you ever considered paying someone just to handle it?

## Closing
14. Is there anything else about how you manage time, notes, and billing that I haven’t asked that you think I should know?
15. If you had a magic wand, what’s the one thing you’d change about how you tie your work to getting paid?
